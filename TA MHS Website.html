WHO WE ARE

BUILDING THE NEXT GENERATION OF INVESTORS

Target Alpha at Maple High School is a student-run finance and business club dedicated to giving high schoolers real-world exposure to investing, strategy, and entrepreneurship.

Whether you have zero experience or you're already tracking your portfolio — we have a competition for you. Our three flagship events put your skills to the test in stock trading, case analysis, and business pitching.

High school students with minimal investing experience will be able to compete in a stock trading simulation on Investopedia. Competitors compete individually — the person with the greatest portfolio value at the end wins!

WHAT WE OFFER

THREE WAYS
TO COMPETE

01
Individual

STOCK TRADING COMPETITION

Trade stocks on Investopedia's simulation platform. No real money — all real strategy. The competitor with the highest portfolio value at the end of the trading period wins. Anyone can enter, even with zero experience.

🏆 How to Win: Diversify your portfolio, research trending sectors, manage risk with stop-losses, and don't panic sell. Stay consistent and track your picks daily.
02
Team

CASE COMPETITION

Analyze a real-world business problem and present your strategic solution to a panel of judges. Teams must research, synthesize, and recommend actionable strategies within a tight timeline.

🏆 How to Win: Structure your solution using frameworks (SWOT, Porter's 5 Forces). Lead with a clear recommendation, back it with data, and rehearse your delivery. Judges love confidence and clarity.
03
Team / Individual

PITCH COMPETITION

Build a business idea from scratch and pitch it to a panel like real investors. You'll need a problem, solution, target market, revenue model, and a compelling story. Think Dragon's Den meets high school.

🏆 How to Win: Tell a story — start with the problem, make judges feel it. Use visuals, be specific about your market size, and practice your pitch until it feels natural. Q&A prep is essential.
